Week 01

Introduction

April 2026

First week of DFAB. We got the rundown of what digital fabrication actually covers — CAD, additive manufacturing, laser cutting — plus a tour of the FabLab (T1442) and the machines we'd be trusted (and occasionally not trusted) with. The big takeaway: everything in this module is design → make → test → iterate, and the journal you're reading is the paper trail.

No screenshots survived week 1 — lesson learned. Everything from week 3 onwards is documented as it happened.
Week 03–04

3D CAD — learning Fusion 360

8 – 15 May 2026

Two weeks of getting comfortable in Fusion 360. Week 3 was all about disciplined sketching: dimensions, constraints, and construction geometry before any 3D happens. The practice sketch below is fully constrained — Ø70/Ø40 circles, a 60 mm arc profile and a slotted rectangle, all locked down with dimensions instead of eyeballing.

From there it was sketch → extrude: a spanner with hex and pentagon cutouts, a rounded plate with through-holes, and a personalised name tag with embossed text and a keyring loop — my first "actually want to print this" moment.

Week 05

3D Printing

late May 2026

CAD models finally met the printers. We covered FDM slicing, layer heights, supports, and the thing that would come to define my CA1: tolerances. A model that looks perfect in Fusion means nothing if the lid doesn't close or the port cutout sits 2 mm off. This week rolled straight into the CA1 brief — design, print and iterate a functional housing.

CA1 Project

Housing for the ESP32-C3

submitted 5 June 2026

BriefDesign and 3D-print a housing for the ESP32-C3 microcontroller mounted on its expansion board — ports accessible, board protected, lid removable, and ideally a way to press the boot/reset buttons without opening the case.

Measure twiceThe board came out to 36 × 22 × 18 mm (L×W×H). With a +1.5 mm tolerance the internal cavity became 37.5 × 23.5 × 19.5 mm, with 2 mm walls throughout.

37.5 × 23.5 × 19.5internal cavity, mm (+1.5 tolerance)
2 mmwall thickness · 1 mm cap inner lip
USB-C 9 × 4port cutout, mm (+0.5 tolerance)
Ø3 mm button7 mm from top · 15 mm from side walls

IterateVersion 1 proved the shell concept but the pin port sat too far left and the cap's inner lip was so tight it needed pliers. Version 1.2 moved the pin port right and offset the lip further. Along the way: bad tolerances, printing errors, and a snap-in clip mechanism that never quite snapped — I dropped the clip idea and kept the friction-fit lip with flexible press-down nubs for the buttons.

  • Pin port too far left → moved pin port right (2 Jun)
  • Cap inner lip fit too tight → offset inner lip further (4 Jun)
  • Redo clip / snap-in lip — dropped in favour of friction fit

FinalThe final version: friction-fit lid with two compliant press-down arms over the boot/reset buttons, USB-C and connector ports lined up first try, and a matte textured finish. Submitted with the physical prototype to FabLab T1442 and the process deck to Brightspace on 5 June.
